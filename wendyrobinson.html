Robinson co-starred in Focus Features feature film, Something New, directed by Sanaa Hamri, a respected director, who made her feature directorial debut on this movie; Rebound, opposite Martin Lawrence where she portrayed the love interest of Martin Lawrence; Two Can Play That Game opposite Morris Chestnut, Vivica A. Fox, Monique and Anthony Anderson; Miss Congeniality starring Sandra Bullock; With Or Without You opposite Cynda Williams and Guy Torry. Robinson also appeared in the zany comedy, Ringmaster starring Jerry Springer, A Thin Line Between Love and Hate opposite Martin Lawrence and The Walking Dead opposite Eddie Griffin.

Recently on television, Robinson checked in Seattle Grace Mercy West Hospital as a guest-star in ABC’s hit drama, Grey’s Anatomy. She received three NAACP Image Award Nominations for Best Actress in a Comedy for her work on The Steve Harvey Show. In addition, she landed a recurring role on ABC’s All of Us, as well as a series regular on Fox’s variety sketch show, Cedric the Entertainer Presents! and NBC’s Minor Adjustments. In addition, Robinson has held additional recurring roles on NYPD Blue, CBS’s Yes, Dear, Fox’s Getting Personal, UPN’s The Parker’s and One on One, as well as ABC’s hit animated show, The Proud Family.

Performing in front of a live audience is one of the most unique experiences in the Theatre Arts. Robinson lights the silver screen and television screen, but she is a burst of solid energy on a theatre stage. Recently, Robinson starred in the staged production of 'Extremities' at the National Black Theater Festival in Winston-Salem, N.C. opposite Dorian Wilson (The Parker’s) directed by Brent Jennings.

Wendy gave an earth-shaking performance in the story of an intended rape victim who manages to escape from her attacker but her worst fears are realized when, alone one day in the house, her attacker visits and attempts again to rape her. Circumstances allow her not only to resist the attack, but also to turn the tables and lock him away.

Robinson also co-starred opposite recording artist, Brian McKnight in Eric Jerome Dickey’s novel, “Cheaters,” and received rave reviews and sold out performances across the country. She has performed in a number of well received productions to include The Vagina Monologues (V-Day-L.A.), Black Woman’s Blues, Agnes of God, A Midsummer’s Night Dream, The Colored Museum and Vanities for which she received an NAACP Theatre Award for Best Choreographer and Best Actress Nomination.

In 1995, Wendy responded to her inner voice to help teens seek out their goals in the arts. As co-founder of Amazing Grace Conservatory (AGC), a theatrical training institute in Los Angeles, Robinson recently relocated to a new studio where she and her staff are dedicated to “building self-esteem through self-expression. AGC is a highly respected training ground for young actors and performers and it is revealed in their performance at the end of each semester. Many of the students who have attended the institute are currently working in the entertainment industry and/or attending college to pursue a degree in the Arts.

In addition to her work in the community, Robinson serves as the Celebrity Spokesperson for the Los Angeles Black Business Expo & Trade Show. She is the recipient of various honors to include , the 2009 Jenesse Center Silver Rose Honoree for her equally brilliant work in nurturing young people to become self sufficient through professional training in the performing arts. She is also the recipient of the Howard University Distinguished Alumni Award, Disneyland & KJLH Proven Achiever Award, including a Woman of Courage Honoree.

Robinson currently resides in Los Angeles, California with her husband and three dogs.
